Treatment
  • Notify surgeon
  • Stop nitrous oxide and volatile agents
  • Increase O2 to 100%
  • Stop air entrainment: find air entry point, stop source, and limit further entry
    • Flood wound with irrigation/soaked saline dressing
    • Turn off all pressurized gas sources: laparoscope, endoscope
    • Place bone wax or cement on exposed bone edges
  • Check for open venous lines or air in IV tubing
  • Lower surgical site below level of heart (if possible)
  • Perform Valsalva on patient using hand ventilation
  • Consider:
    • Compress jugular veins intermittently if head or cranial case
    • Aspirate from central venous catheter
  • Hemodynamic support if hypotensive:
    • Give epinephrine 1-10 MICROgrams/kg, followed by infusion of epinephrine 0.02-1 MICROgrams/kg/min or norepinephrine 0.05-2 MICROgrams/kg/min
    • Give epinephrine MICROgrams (1-10 MICROgrams/kg), followed by infusion of epinephrine 0.02-1 MICROgrams/kg/min or norepinephrine 0.05-2 MICROgrams/kg/min
    • Chest compressions: 100-120/min to force air through lock, even if not in cardiac arrest
    • If available, call for Transesophageal Echocardiography
    • Consider ECMO
  • Consider DDx
