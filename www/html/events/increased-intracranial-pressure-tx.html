Treatment
  • If GCS < 9, respiratory distress, hemodynamic instability:
    • Secure airway
    • Provide sedation prior to transport
  • Keep PaCO2 30-35 mmHg and PaO2 > 60 mmHg
  • Maintain cerebral perfusion pressure (discuss goal CPP with team)
  • Discuss target ICP with neurosurgery, will often want ICP > 20
  • Use vasopressors (phenylephrine or NOREPInephrine) as needed to maintain BP and CPP
  • Consider head of bed at 30°
  • Hypertonic saline (3% saline via central venous catheter) 1-5 mL/kg over 20 min, then 0.1-2 mL/kg/hour; goal ICP < 20 mmHg Hypertonic saline (3% saline via central venous catheter) mL (1-5 mL/kg) over 20 min, then mL/hour (0.1-2 mL/kg/hour); goal ICP < 20 mmHg
    • Monitor serum sodium
    • Keep osmolarity < 360 mOsm/L
  • If hypertonic saline not available, can give mannitol 0.25-1 g/kg, over 20 minutes to decrease ICP
  • If hypertonic saline not available, can give mannitol g (0.25-1 g/kg), over 20 minutes to decrease ICP
  • Consider furosemide 1-2 mg/kg (starting MAX 20 mg) to decrease ICP
  • Consider furosemide mg (1-2 mg/kg, starting MAX 20 mg) to decrease ICP
  • Consider seizure prophylaxis: Keppra (levetiracetam) 10-30 mg/kg IV (MAX 2500 mg)
  • Consider seizure prophylaxis: Keppra (levetiracetam) mg (10-30 mg/kg, MAX 2500 mg) IV
  • Refractory elevated ICP treatment, consider:
    • Barbiturate coma
    • Paralysis with non-depolarizing agent
  • AVOID:
    • Compression of neck vessels
    • Hyperthermia
    • Hyperglycemia & dextrose containing solutions (maintain glucose level < 200 mg/dL)
