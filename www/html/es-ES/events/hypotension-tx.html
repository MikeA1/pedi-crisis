Tratamiento
  • In pediatrics, hypotension is almost always treated by correcting hypovolemia or decreasing the anesthetic agent
  • Ensure oxygenation/ ventilation
  • Turn anesthetic agents down or off
  • Check cuff size and transducer position
  • If severe and/or persistent, consider placing arterial line if not already present
Etiology (Pick One):
  • Expand circulating blood volume (administer fluids rapidly, consider albumin)
  • Trendelenberg position
  • Place or replace IV; consider intraosseous line
  • Causes: hypovolemia, vasodilation, impaired venous return, tamponade, pulmonary embolism, hypocalcemia/blood product administration, IVC compression (prone, obese, surgical), pneumothorax/pneumoperitoneum, and increased PIP or PEEP
  • Start inotrope infusion, as needed
    • Dopamine 2-20 MICROgramos/kg/min infusion IV
    • Bolus epinephrine 1-10 MICROgramos/kg IV/IO, consider infusion 0.05-1 MICROgramos/kg/min IV
    • Bolus epinephrine MICROgramos IV/IO (1-10 MICROgramos/kg IV/IO), consider infusion 0.05-1 MICROgramos/kg/min IV
  • Consider calcium IV
  • Review ECG for rhythm disturbances or ischemia
  • Send ABG, Hgb, electrolytes
  • Causes: negative inotropic drugs (anesthetic agents), arrhythmias, HYPOXIA, and heart failure (ischemia)
  • Start vasopressor infusion
    • Consider infusion of norepinephrine 0.05-2 MICROgramos/kg/min IV
    • Bolus phenylephrine 1-20 MICROgramos/kg IV/IO, consider infusion 0.1-2 MICROgramos/kg/min IV
    • Bolus phenylephrine MICROgramos IV/IO (1-20 MICROgramos/kg), consider infusion 0.1-2 MICROgramos/kg/min IV
  • Administer steroids for adrenal crisis
  • Causes: drug-induced vasodilation, sepsis, ANAFILAXIA, adrenal crisis, adrenal insufficiency, hypocalcemia, thyroid crisis
